Wind Energy

How does wind power work?

  • Wind power converts the kinetic energy of air in motion into electricity by rotating rotor blades in modern wind turbines.

Global wind energy growth

  • Since 2000, wind power capacity has surged, driven by R&D, supportive policies, and declining costs, with global installed capacity jumping from 7.5 GW in 1997 to approximately 733 GW by 2018.

Evolution of wind technology

  • Wind turbine capacity has increased over time, with modern turbines boasting capacities of 3-4 MW onshore and 8-12 MW offshore, harnessing the wind's power more efficiently.

Hydroelectric Power

How does hydroelectric energy work?

  • Hydroelectric energy harnesses the force of moving water, like water flowing over a waterfall, to generate electricity. This method has been used for centuries.

Types of Hydroelectric Energy Plants

  • Impoundment Facilities: Utilize dams to control water flow from a reservoir, releasing water to flow through turbines.
  • Diversion Facilities: Channel river water toward turbines through canals, bypassing the need for dams.
  • Pumped-Storage Facilities: Store surplus energy from sources like solar and wind by pumping water uphill to a higher reservoir for future electricity generation.

Global Usage of Hydroelectric Energy

  • Hydroelectricity is the most common renewable electricity source globally, with China leading in production, followed by the United States, Brazil, and others. About 71% of renewable electricity worldwide comes from hydropower.

Reference: National Geographic - Hydroelectric Energy

Biomass Energy

What is biomass?

  • Biomass is renewable organic material derived from plants and animals, containing stored chemical energy from the sun.

Usage of biomass

  • Biomass is utilized for heating, electricity generation, and as a transportation fuel. It's particularly vital in developing countries for cooking and heating.

Conversion methods

  • Biomass can be converted to energy through direct combustion, thermochemical conversion, chemical conversion, and biological conversion processes.
